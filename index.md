# Terry Ruskoski

I am interested in making science and healthcare function more effectively—especially in the spaces where complex work, limited resources, and human factors intersect. I have always approached problems through a systems lens, and my training in biochemistry provided a rigorous environment to apply and refine that perspective. Over time, this focus extended beyond experimental work to how laboratory systems actually operate: where they break down, how they can be improved, and how better structure can support both the people doing the work and the outcomes they are trying to achieve.

I use systems-based approaches such as continuous improvement and Lean methodologies to identify root causes, reduce inefficiencies, and strengthen reliability. I am particularly motivated by opportunities to translate complex scientific work into practical, well-functioning systems that are easier to use, more consistent, and better aligned with real-world needs.
